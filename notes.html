My notes for physics

It is not a trivial task for us math students to learn physics. To learn physics (or any natural science), we need to grasp the physical intuition of the topic, while as math students, we hope we can be mathematically clear at least in some sense, if we can't have mathematical rigor.

It is somewhat difficult to have both mathematical clarity (or rigor) and scientific intuition at the same time. But it's very important that we try to have them both. On the one hand, without physical intuition, it'll be almost impossible to do things scientifically beneficial with only mathematical understandings. On the other hand, mathematical rigor (or trying to be mathematical rigorous) can help make things become more clear.
